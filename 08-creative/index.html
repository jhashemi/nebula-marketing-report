Plain definitions

MetricPlain EnglishHow to read it
CTROf every 100 people who see the ad, how many click.Higher is better. B2B legal-tech median ≈ 1.5%.
CPMWhat it costs to show the ad to 1,000 people.Lower is better. Display $5–$15. LinkedIn $35–$80.
CPCWhat we pay per click.Calculated as CPM ÷ (CTR × 10).
CPL / CPACost per qualified lead / signed deal.The number that actually matters.

6 · TikTok

NOT VIABLE — YEAR ONE The audience is wrong for our ICP. Even “LawTok” is dominated by personal-injury and immigration content; the IP-litigation buyer doesn't consume professional content there.

  1. Direct response? No — 0.02% conversion in B2B-legal experiments.
  2. Brand awareness? Possibly — at $35 CPM with no targetable audience, the math is identical to LinkedIn at half the trust.
  3. Recruiting? Maybe later — once we hire patent agents, TikTok could surface non-traditional candidates. Not Year-1.

Decision: $0 to TikTok in months 1–9. Reassess at month 10.

Benchmark summary

Quality bar — every creative passes these 5 rules

  1. Name a number. “$635M.” “205 lawsuits.” “10 minutes.” Numbers stop the scroll.
  2. Name a competitor or a current pain. “Replace your PatSnap seat.”
  3. Promise a verb, not a feature. “Find prior art.” not “Semantic embedding-based retrieval.”
  4. Word invalidity or prior art in first 5 words. Self-qualifies the click.
  5. Low-commitment CTA. “Read the case study” outperforms “Book a demo” 4:1 on first touch.
