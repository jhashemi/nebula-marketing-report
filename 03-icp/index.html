Chapter 03 · Ideal Customer Profile

Who exactly
writes the check.

The Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) is the description of the buyer who gets the most value from Nebula and is easiest to reach. Marketing dollars only work when they hit this person. Everyone else is noise.

Plain definition of the ICP

Companies that get sued (or sue) over patents — specifically over standard-essential patents (SEPs), which are patents declared essential for using a technology standard like 5G, Wi-Fi, or H.265 video. When a company is dragged into a patent suit, lawyers must quickly find prior art: older publications or products that show the patented idea wasn't actually new. Today they pay outside firms $50k–$300k per search and wait 4–8 weeks. Nebula does it in minutes.

The three personas who actually buy

Persona A — Chief IP Counsel Economic Buyer

  • Title variants: Chief IP Counsel, VP Intellectual Property, Head of Patent Strategy
  • Reports to: General Counsel
  • Budget authority: Owns the $5M–$25M annual outside-counsel budget
  • Age / tenure: 45–60, 15+ years patent practice
  • Day looks like: Litigation status reviews, FRAND license negotiations, board updates on IP risk

What they want

Predictable spend. Defensible decisions. A tool the partners at their outside firm will respect, not snicker at. They sign deals that make their internal team look smarter, not cheaper.

Persona B — Senior Patent Litigator Champion

  • Title variants: Senior Counsel - Litigation, Patent Litigation Lead, Director of Enforcement
  • Reports to: Chief IP Counsel
  • Why they matter: They live inside active cases. They feel the pain hourly. They become Nebula's internal evangelist.
  • Trigger event: Just got served a complaint or about to file one. Has a 21-day clock.

What they want

Speed. They need three solid invalidity references by Friday. If Nebula gives them one extra reference outside firms missed, they will personally get the contract signed.

Persona D — Prior-Art Analyst End User

  • Title variants: Patent Analyst, IP Research Specialist, Senior Examiner (in-house)
  • Reports to: Patent Litigator
  • Pain quote: “I've been searching the same Boolean string in PatSnap for 3 days. Half my hits are translated machine-text from 1998 KIPO pubs.”
  • Influence: Tells the litigator if the tool actually works on real cases.

What they want

Tool that doesn't waste their time. Fewer false-positive hits. Translated foreign-language prior art ranked by semantic relevance, not keyword overlap.

Anti-ICP — who we do NOT sell to

  • Solo IP boutiques (<10 attorneys) — pricing-sensitive, slow procurement, ARPA too low.
  • Universities and research orgs — they file patents, they don't defend against them. Wrong jobs-to-be-done.
  • Patent-assertion entities (NPEs / “trolls”) — the WSOU narrative makes us their enemy. Selling to them poisons the brand with the defendant base who is our actual ICP.
  • Inventors / startups pre-Series-B — no litigation exposure yet, no budget, no pain.
