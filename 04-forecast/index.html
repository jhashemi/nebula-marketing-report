Per-channel CPA, 30-day curve

Meta begins the horizon at $40 CPA and drifts to $40.27 — essentially flat. Google starts hot at $49.33, drops to a $43.59 mean as the auction model adapts to budget pacing. LinkedIn is the cost story: $78.71 opening day, $73.79 close, mean $70.21 — consistently 60–80% more expensive than Meta. The XReg covariate (daily budget + competition index + 7-day seasonality) explains 60% of the per-day variance; the Markov regime-switching component the remaining 40%.

Meta with confidence band

The quantile band is tight in the first week, widens slightly through D15, then re-tightens as seasonality stabilizes. P10–P90 spread is roughly 18% of the median — well within actionable bounds for budget pacing decisions. This is the band the optimizer treats as actionable; anything wider would default the recommendation to "hold and re-forecast."
