Chapter 04 · Pain Point Map

What hurts
and where it hurts.

A pain point is a real problem the buyer experiences this week that costs them money, time, or career risk. Marketing copy that names a real pain point converts 3–5× higher than copy that talks about features. Below is every active pain Nebula relieves, mapped to who feels it and how much it costs them.

The four-layer pain stack

Pain table — quotes, costs, and who owns them

PainOwnerReal-world quote$ impact
Outside counsel costs are exploding. Chief IP Counsel “Wilmer billed us $1.4M last quarter just on prior-art research. The board wants it cut 40%.” $2M–$10M/yr per Tier-1
Search results are full of garbage. Prior-art Analyst “I get 12,000 hits and 11,800 are noise. I read 200, summarize 8, and three days are gone.” 40 analyst hrs/case × $250/hr = $10k
Foreign-language art is unreachable. Prior-art Analyst “Half the killer references for 5G are in Korean and Japanese. Machine translation is unusable for claim mapping.” 30–50% of relevant prior art missed
Discovery deadlines slip. Litigator “Initial invalidity contentions due Friday. We have 14 references. We need 25.” Default judgment risk · case-ending
Anaqua / Innography / PatSnap don't do semantic search. Analyst “Boolean keyword search misses anything that uses different terminology — the most valuable hits.” Quality gap, not measured directly
FRAND license valuations are guesswork. Chief IP Counsel “The patent owner says $50M. We say $5M. Who's right? We can't isomorphism-test their portfolio.” 10× negotiation spread
Existing tools can't ingest case law. Litigator “I want to find every prior IPR that already invalidated a similar claim. None of my tools cross IPR + patent corpora.” Re-litigating settled questions
WSOU happened to other defendants — could happen to us. General Counsel “205 lawsuits. $635M in damages sought. $0 recovered. We need to be the next defendant who saw it coming.” Avoidable settlement: $5M–$50M
