What the data says

We ran the full CampaignForge pipeline against a real ICP-matched B2B legal-tech profile — TimesFM 2.5 with XReg covariates on GPU, an SMT-solver budget allocator, and a creative-strategy bridge that turns the forecast into ad copy and budget guardrails. The math is in the dashboard. The story is in the numbers it produces.

First: Meta is the surprise. The model ranks Meta's forecasted CPA at $48.52 against a target of $54.92 — a 12% margin of safety — and the optimizer recommends scaling Meta by 20% inside the conservative envelope. That contradicts the conventional wisdom that B2B legal-tech only works on LinkedIn. It shouldn't. LinkedIn's CPA forecasts at $88.92, nearly 2× Meta's, and the conversion rate is 1.55% versus Meta's 2.53%.

Second: the lawsuit narrative is the budget. WSOU Investments has filed 205 patent suits seeking $635M in damages and recovered $0. Every plaintiff who took on a Big Tech defendant on faulty SEP claims wrote a six-figure check to the wrong firm. Nebula's Patent Intelligence Engine reads the standards-essential patent record in 24 hours and tells you, before filing, whether you have a case. That is the message. Everything else — channels, creatives, funnels — is plumbing.

Third: the SMT optimizer prefers the conservative tier at 80.6% confidence, not the aggressive one. The model is telling us not to crowd the auction. Buy 70% of full budget, scale Meta, hold LinkedIn at parity, and keep $87k as a learning reserve for retargeting and lookalike expansion in days 15–30.
