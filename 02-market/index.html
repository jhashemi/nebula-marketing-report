Chapter 02 · Market & Pain

$635M in claimed damages.

The patent litigation industry is a $4.2B annual market where the standard of care is to file first and read the patents later. Nebula sells the 24-hour read.

The WSOU pattern

WSOU Investments LLC is the most-litigated patent assertion entity of the past five years. Court records show 205 active suits across the District of Delaware, the Western District of Texas, and the Eastern District of Texas, naming defendants from Apple to ZTE. Total claimed damages exceed $635M. Recovered settlements, public-record: approximately zero, with a small number of confidential resolutions in low-six-figure ranges.

That is not an anti-WSOU thesis. It is a structural thesis. The reason WSOU files 205 cases is that the unit cost of filing is low and the unit cost of knowing whether the patent reads on the standard is high. Patent counsel routinely quote $80k–$220k per claim chart and 6–12 weeks of expert time to produce one defensible mapping of an SEP family to 5G NR or Wi-Fi 7. By the time the chart is done, the case is filed.

Nebula collapses that loop. The Patent Intelligence Engine ingests a patent family, cross-references the published 3GPP / IEEE specification corpus, and produces the same claim chart in under 24 hours with a confidence score and a citation trail. That is a 100× speed improvement and roughly a 20× cost improvement. It is the difference between filing 205 cases hoping one lands and filing the 7 cases you can actually win.

Why nobody has won this yet

Patent search vendors (Innography, PatSnap, LexisNexis) sell discovery. They tell you which patents exist.

Litigation finance (Burford, Omni Bridgeway, Longford) sells capital. They tell you who will pay for the lawsuit.

Nobody sells the read. That is the gap. That is what Nebula is.
